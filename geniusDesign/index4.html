The Genius of Design IV

Better Living Through Chemistry

  • "In the era of unprecedented consumer choice, innovative marketing became part of a designer's success. Selling the merchandise through home parties, rather than shops, brought it to life. The parties weren't just demonstrating a product, they put it at the center of an aspirational lifestyle."

    在消费者拥有选择权的年代，创新性营销成为推动设计走向成功的一个关键因素。通过家庭聚会将产品代入你的生活，而不是只依靠商场销售。这个聚会不只是为了展示产品，更要勾画出一种让人梦寐以求的生活方式。

    Tupperware by Earl Tupper, 1946

  • "It was utterly radical. Nothing like it had been seen before. As radios, record players came along they came along absolutely with that aesthetic. You have a lighter colour wood, in terms of beech. You have this radical use of a single sheet of folded steel. Of course, you have the clear plastic lid. Clear use of circles for twisting dials, of rectangular buttons for press buttons with concave surfaces inviting the finger to push. The celebration of the object for its own right, rather than it being an object of which you needed to be. The instant clarity of Dieter's designs clearly obeys the modernist mantra that form should follow function. Theintegrity of these designs was more than just pleasing to the eye, can also help the democracy, that could help altogether to make a new society."

    它显得异常激进。从未有过类似的产品整合了收音机和录音机功能，充满决定美学意味。它利用了一种轻质的彩色木料山毛榉木，有一块带转角的金属板壳，以及洁净的塑料盖子。干净的圆形旋钮是用来调音的，而按钮则设计成矩形。下凹的表面使人们喜欢按下去的感觉。它的成功在于恰当地表现了其功能，不会使你觉得无所适从。简洁明快，正式迪特尔的设计语言，他严格遵循着形式追随功能的现代主义法则。这种诚实设计的意义已不局限于悦目的外表，也有助于民主精神的传播，有助于建立起一个新的社会。

    SK4 Record Player/Radio by Dieter Rams & Hans Gugelot, 1956

  • Motorway Signage by Jock Kinneir & Margaret Calvert, 1958

  • "... and also with a magnifying glass in the summer you could burn holes in it, you can burn your name into the back of it. It's a socialist concept that low cost, good design is great for everybody, but it's also a reaction to over-ornament and its association with the kind of bourgeois living, and it's the idea that good, soimple design is fit for purpose and fit for everybody and this chair is that."

    在椅子后面挖一个洞，那就相当于设计师的签名。低价是一种社会道德，好的设计师应该服务大众。这与装饰繁杂的上流社会物品形成了鲜明的对比。民主设计坚信优良的产品，要简介而且大众，就像这把椅子一样。

    'Polyprop' Chair by Robn Day, 1963

  • "I don't mind so much about what public wants. It is always difficult to imterpret what people want, following marketing research, I mean. I prefer to use intuition and sensibility. We use a different method of creation for objects, which is an artistic system, an artistic method."

    我不那么关注大众想要什么。搞清楚大家究竟想要些什么是很困难的。仅仅依靠市场调查是不够的。我更愿意相信直觉以及灵感。我们用一种全新的方法艺术性地创造物品。

    Bombe Tea and Coffee Service by Carlo Alessi, 1945

  • "Many of these objects are about a very self-conscious way of constructing a new lifestyle, a lifestyle that isn't about inheriting your parents' furniture. It's about a complete shift in generational understanding of everyday living."

    这些产品构建起一种自我意识浓烈的生活方式，一种抛弃掉上一辈传下的老古董的生活方式，这是新一代人对生活态度的彻底转变。

  • "The Panton chair is a complete paradox because while on the one hand it is, withiout any doubt, a design classic, It has virtually no constract application. Can you see this chair in a hospital? Can you see this chair in an office? They were never going to earn back their costs for the amount of investment that went in. It was basically a flyer they took and it didn't make it."

    潘顿椅绝对是个矛盾体。因为无可争议的，它是设计界的经典，实际上没有合同愿意签下这把椅子。你在医院里看过这种椅子吗？你在办公室见过这种椅子吗？实际上在它们身上的投资都是无法收回的，基本上它只是个扔进水里的石子，有去无回。

    S Chair by Verner Panton, 1959-67
