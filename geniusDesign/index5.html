The Genius of Design V

Objects of Desire

  • Lockheed Lounge by Marc Newson, 1986, 1936

  • Joy Division by Peter Saville, 1979

  • "... and I quite liked the idea of having a Fantin-Latour. And I saw these in a way kind of 'pop' products as a way if having that. And it IS about acquisition. It's dirven by aspiration and a personal desire to have some things. And following on from centuries of people NOT having things, I mean, it's a wonder of the post-war period that people now have the opportunity to have things. It's brilliant. And if those things make them feel better about themselves, and without a doubt the kind of design object brings with it this sense of self-enhancement, and the sense of knowing more than you did before, these are all positive things."

    我很喜欢将 Fantin-Latour 的画用于设计的想法。这样，稀有的名画也成了可拥有的流行设计品。就是这种据为己有的感觉，它来源于人类深层次的与生俱来的占有欲。几个世纪以来人们的占有欲都被压抑着，我觉得这就是要赞美战后时代的原因。人们现在有权利去战友心爱之物了，多棒啊。这样的产品让人们自我感觉更好，毋庸置疑，这类设计品可以强化人的自我意识，让你感觉自己的世界变得更加丰富多彩，这意义非凡。

    New Order - Power, Corruption and Lies by Peter Saville, 1983

  • "Modernism sought to make the world a better place for all. Memphis prefigured a new idea, where the collective good made way for individual desire. And that meant the rise of a new kind of designed object."

    现代主义力图总揽全局式地改善这个世界。孟菲斯却提出了一个新的观点，那就是集体主义要让位于个体诉求。那也意味着一种新的设计风潮的兴起。

    Carlton Bookcase by Ettore Scottsass, 1981

  • "For some, this cultural upgrade was distancing design from its unique ability to connect form and function. Degrading it into a tool for selling us stuff. Stuff we may want, but may or may not need. For others it was redefining the very notion of function itself."

    对一些人来说，这种审美的进化却使得设计失去了那种将形式与功能统一起来的功用。设计沦为促销的手段，仅是做出一些我们想要，但不一定用到的东西。对另一些人来说，这种设计重新定义了功能这个词。

    Kettle by Michael Graves, 1985

  • "But since this time there is Sigmund Freud. There is the French philosopher Lacan, and we know that there is not only materialistic paameters, there is immaturial parameters, like poetry, humour, sex and things like that. We can say I am a post-Freudian functionalist. But these functions now are more richer than before, because we know more about us. That's the difference."

    但后来出现了西格蒙德弗洛伊德，然后又出现了法国哲学家拉冈。我意识到我们不仅要关注功能上的具体参数，更有一些情感上的因素，比如诗意，幽默感，性感这些东西。所以我应该算是后弗洛伊德式的功能主义者。但这些功能主义比以前的更丰富，因为我们更多地探讨了人性化，这就是两者的区别。

    Juicy Salif by Philippe Starck, 1990

  • "Our view is exactly the opposite. We use the design to really come to the lowest price, so people can afford to buy things. But I just think the sense to which the whole experience of being an IKEA customer is so diminished, to visit an IKEA store is to experience sort of humiliation and degradation and which is only normally available in a psychiatric hospital or an airport. But loathe it, or love it, it's curious that in the age of the individual, such a depersonalized consumer experience should become so successful."

    宜家的设计理念恰恰相反，我们的设计是为了尽可能让产品的价格更加低廉，以便更多的人们买得起宜家家具。但我认为在宜家购物的整个过程中，都有一种明显的被忽视的感觉，光顾宜家门店就伴随着一种倍羞辱和丢脸的感觉。这感觉在精神病院和机场司空见惯。但无论你是喜欢它，还是讨厌它，在这样一个强调个性化的年代，这样一种与个性化相违背的购物体验却仍然取得了相当大的成功。

    Lovet Table, Gillis Lundgren, 1956

  • "Making design 'user-friendly' has become essential to making once unimaginably complex technology part of the fabric of everyday life."

    设计中提倡的“界面友好原则”，在将过去非常复杂的技术转化为普通生活的一部分的过程中，起到了至关重要的作用。

    Macintosh by Apple Computer. Inc. , 1984

  • "So, to cut a long story short, the principles of user-centered design have made some of the most complex consumer products man has ever made an essential part of our everyday world, through design that aims to be all but invisible."

    长话短说，以用户为中心的设计，旨在将人类研制出的最复杂的消费产品变成我们日常生活中的重要组成部分。设计已渗透进生活的方方面面，却不被人注意到。

    Computer Mouse by Stanford Research Institude, 1963

  • iPod by Jonathan Ive & Apple Design Team, 2001

  • "With Cradle to Cradle design, you get to be ecological AND keep shopping."

    利用“从摇篮到摇篮”设计，你可以保持生态环境友好，并且，无需控制消费。

    Think Chair by Glen Oliver Low, 2008
